Burning waste, especially plastics and other synthetic materials, releases a toxic concoction

of pollutants into the air. The combustion of plastics generates harmful gases such as dioxins,

furans, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs). When these pollutants are released into the atmosphere,

they can travel over long distances, affecting both local and regional air quality.


Dioxins and Furans: The Unseen Threats

Dioxins and furans are highly toxic compounds that form during the incomplete combustion of waste.

These persistent organic pollutants are known for their harmful effects on human health and the environment.

Prolonged exposure to dioxins has been linked to various health issues, including reproductive and developmental problems, damage to the immune system, and an increased risk of cancer.
